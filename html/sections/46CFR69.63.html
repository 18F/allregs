CFR /  Title 46  /  Part 69  /  Sec. 69.63 Net tonnage.

Net tonnage (NT) is determined by the formula:

[GRAPHIC] [TIFF OMITTED] TR30SE97.006

in which: Vc = total volume of cargo spaces in cubic meters. K2 = 0.2 + 0.02 log10 Vc. [GRAPHIC] [TIFF OMITTED] TR30SE97.007 D = molded depth amidships in meters, as ``molded depth'' is defined in

Sec. 69.53. d = molded draft amidships in meters, as ``molded draft'' is defined in

Sec. 69.53. N1 = number of passengers in cabins with not more than eight

berths, as ``passenger'' is defined in Sec. 69.53. N2 = number of other passengers, as ``passenger'' is defined

in Sec. 69.53. GT = gross tonnage as determined under Sec. 69.57. N1 plus N2 must equal the total number of

passengers the vessel is permitted to carry as indicated on

the ship's Passenger Certificate. If N1 plus

N2 is less than 13, both N1 and

N2 are zero.

[GRAPHIC] [TIFF OMITTED] TR30SE97.008

NT must not be less than 0.30 GT. [CGD 97-057, 62 FR 51045, Sept. 30, 1997]
