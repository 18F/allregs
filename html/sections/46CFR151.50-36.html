CFR /  Title 46  /  Part 151  /  Sec. 151.50-36 Argon or nitrogen.

(a) A cargo tank that contains argon or nitrogen and that has a maximum allowable working pressure of 172 kPa (25 psig) or greater must have one of the following arrangements:

(1) A refrigeration system that keeps the tank pressure below the safety relief valve operating pressure when ambient temperatures are 46 [deg]C (115 [deg]F) air and 32 [deg]C (90 [deg]F) water.

(2) A relief valve or pressure control valve that maintains the tank pressure below the setting of the tank's required safety relief valve in ambient temperatures of 46 [deg]C (115 [deg]F) air and 32 [deg]C (90 [deg]F) water.

(b) A cargo tank with a maximum allowable working pressure of less than 172 kPa (25 psig) is approved by the Commandant (CG-ENG) on a case by case basis.

(c) Section 151.50-30 also applies to the carriage of argon or nitrogen. [CGD 88-100, 54 FR 40040, Sept. 29, 1989]
