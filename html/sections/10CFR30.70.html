Note 1: Many radioisotopes disintegrate into isotopes which are also radioactive. In expressing the

concentrations in Schedule A, the activity stated is that of the parent isotope and takes into account the

daughters. Note 2: For purposes of Sec. 30.14 where there is involved a combination of isotopes, the limit for the

combination should be derived as follows: Determine for each isotope in the product the ratio between the concentration present in the product and the

exempt concentration established in Schedule A for the specific isotope when not in combination. The sum of

such ratios may not exceed ``1'' (i.e., unity). Example: [GRAPHIC] [TIFF OMITTED] TC14NO91.021 [30 FR 8185, June 26, 1965, as amended at 35 FR 3982, Mar. 3, 1970; 38 FR 29314, Oct. 24, 1973; 59 FR 5520, Feb. 7, 1994]
