CFR /  Title 7  /  Part 97  /  Sec. 97.1 General.

Certificates of protection are issued by the Plant Variety Protection office for new, distinct, uniform, and stable varieties of sexually reproduced or tubor propagated plants. Each certificate of plant variety protection certifies that the breeder has the right, during the term of the protection, to prevent others from selling the variety, offering it for sale, reproducing it, importing or exporting it, conditioning it, stocking it, or using it in producing a hybrid or different variety from it, as provided by the Act. [58 FR 42435, Aug. 9, 1993, as amended at 60 FR 17189, Apr. 4, 1995]
