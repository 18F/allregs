(c) Illustration of principles. The provisions of section 802(a)(3) and this section may be illustrated by the following example:

Example. For the taxable year 1960, X, a life insurance company, had taxable investment income of $9,000, gain from operations of $27,000, and subtractions from the policyholders surplus account of $22,000. Based upon these figures, X had life insurance company taxable income of $40,000 for 1960, of which $18,000 was includible under section 802(b) (1) and (2) and $22,000 under section 802(b)(3). Applying the tax imposed by section 802(a)(1) (at rates as in effect for 1960), without regard to the transitional rule of section 802(a)(3), X would have a tax liability of $15,300 ($40,000 multiplied by 52 percent, less $5,500).
