CFR /  Title 29  /  Part 776  /  Sec. 776.6 Coverage not dependent on place of work.

Except for the general geographical limitations discussed in Sec. 776.7, the Act contains no prescription as to the place where the employee must work in order to come within its coverage. It follows that employees otherwise coming within the terms of the Act are entitled to its benefits whether they perform their work at home, in the factory, or elsewhere. \18\ The specific provisions of the Act relative to regulation of homework serve to emphasize this fact. \19\---------------------------------------------------------------------------

\18\ Walling v. American Needlecrafts, 139 F. 2d 60 (C.A. 6); Walling v. Twyeffort Inc., 158 F. 2d 944 (C.A. 2); McComb v. Homeworkers' Handicraft Cooperative, 176 F. 2d 633 (C.A. 4).

\19\ See 6(a)(2); Sec. 11(d). ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
