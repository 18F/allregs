CFR /  Title 37  /  Part 1  /  Sec. 1.956 Patent owner extensions of time in inter partes

The time for taking any action by a patent owner in an inter partes reexamination proceeding will be extended only for sufficient cause and for a reasonable time specified. Any request for such extension must be filed on or before the day on which action by the patent owner is due, but in no case will the mere filing of a request effect any extension. Any request for such extension must be accompanied by the petition fee set forth in Sec. 1.17(g). See Sec. 1.304(a) for extensions of time for filing a notice of appeal to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit. [69 FR 56546, Sept. 21, 2004]
