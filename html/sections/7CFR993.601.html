CFR /  Title 7  /  Part 993  /  Sec. 993.601 More restrictive grade regulation.

(a) shall be the applicable standards set forth in Sec. 993.97 Exhibit A; minimum standards, except that the following revised tolerance allowances shall apply in lieu of the tolerance allowances prescribed in paragraphs I C(2), II C(3), I C(5) and II C(6) of Sec. 993.97 as follows:

(1) The combined tolerance allowance for off-color, inferior meat condition, end cracks, fermentation, skin or flesh damage, scab, burned, mold, imbedded dirt, insect infestation, and decay shall not exceed fifteen percent (15%), except that the first eight percent (8%) of end cracks shall be given one-half value and any additional percentage of end cracks shall be given full value.

(2) The combined tolerance allowance for mold, brown rot, imbedded dirt, insect infestation, and decay shall not exceed five percent (5%), and, within such tolerance, brown rot shall not exceed three percent (3%).

(b) Above parity situations. Whenever the estimated season average price to producers for prunes exceeds the parity level specified in section 2(1) of the act, the minimum standards set forth in Sec. 993.97 shall apply in their entirety. [27 FR 7540, Aug. 1, 1962, as amended at 59 FR 38113, July 27, 1994]

Effective Date Note: At 70 FR 30613, May 27, 2005, Sec. 993.601 was suspended indefinitely.
