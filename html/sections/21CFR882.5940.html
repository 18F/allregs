CFR /  Title 21  /  Part 882  /  Sec. 882.5940 Electroconvulsive therapy device.

(a) Identification. An electroconvulsive therapy device is a device used for treating severe psychiatric disturbances (e.g., severe depression) by inducing in the patient a major motor seizure by applying a brief intense electrical current to the patient's head.

(b) Classification. Class III (premarket approval).

(c) Date PMA or notice of completion of a PDP is required. No effective date has been established of the requirement for premarket approval. See Sec. 882.3. [44 FR 51730, Sept. 4, 1979, as amended at 52 FR 17740, May 11, 1987]
